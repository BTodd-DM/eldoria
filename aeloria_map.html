Merchants Rise
Northwest District · Trade & Craft
Description
The commercial heart of Aeloria. Wide cobbled streets lined with workshops, storefronts, and trading houses. The smell of hot metal from the Ironwell Forge drifts through the quarter. Merchants from across Eldoria maintain offices here. During the day it is loud, bustling, and relentlessly mercantile.
Atmosphere
Energetic by day, quieter at night but never fully dark. The wealthy merchants here have private guards. Occasional guild disputes spill into the streets.
Key NPCs

Durnan Ironwell
Dwarf. Blacksmith. Ironwell Forge. Broad, soot-covered, calm but intense. "You want cheap? Go elsewhere."
Council Connection
Councillor Dara Voss (Merchant Consortium) has her offices here. Her assistant is on the Guilded Veil's payroll.
DM: The Aurum Dominion has been quietly approaching senior merchants in this district about exclusive trade contracts. Vaeloran has leverage over Dara Voss through tax documents. Watch this district as a political flashpoint.
High Spires District
Northeast District · Wealth, Power & Arcana
Description
The most affluent quarter of Aeloria. Home to nobles, wealthy scholars, arcane practitioners, and the powerful. Streets are well-maintained and guarded. Towers of stone rise above the roofline — most notably the Spire of the Silver Eclipse, Vaeloran's iconic landmark. The air feels different here: cleaner, quieter, watched.
Atmosphere
Polished and reserved. Guards are private, professional, and observant. Not hostile to visitors, but scrutinising. Residents value discretion above all.

Ashgate Slums
Southwest District · The City's Underbelly
Description
The roughest district in Aeloria. Cramped buildings lean against each other for support. Alleys twist unpredictably. The smell of ash, old ale, and refuse. But there is a community here — people who know each other, who look out for their own, and who keep secrets as a matter of survival. Not dangerous if you know how to carry yourself. Very dangerous if you don't.

City Hall
City Centre · The Seat of Aelorian Power
Description
The largest building in Aeloria, situated at the exact crossroads of the city's main thoroughfares. A grand stone structure with a tall central spire. Home to the Council of Five, administrative offices, public records, and the offices of the Townmaster. All roads in Aeloria lead here — literally and politically.
Atmosphere
Busy, bureaucratic, and carefully neutral. Everyone here represents something. Conversations are diplomatic by default. The building is impressive — designed to remind visitors of Aeloria's independence.
The Council of Five
Aeloria is governed by a Council of Five seats — Merchant Consortium, Artisans & Guilds, Citizens' Assembly, Scholars & Arcane Assembly, and City Watch & Defence. The party has not yet been formally introduced to its members.

Spire of the Silver Eclipse
High Spires District · Vaeloran's tower · One of Aeloria's most recognisable landmarks
High Spires District · Vaeloran's tower · DM Eyes Only
Description
One of Aeloria's most recognisable landmarks. A spire of pale grey stone that catches moonlight in a way that makes it seem almost to glow. The interior feels larger than the exterior. Swirling orbs of light float between rows of stacked tomes. Mechanical quills jot notes on parchment without hands. Shelves slide quietly to make way as you pass. A faint scent of old paper and candlewax. In one corner, an hourglass drips not sand but shimmering dust that flows upward instead of down.
What the Party Has Seen
Ground floor: Reception, waiting area, Taren Myles's workspace.
Upper floors: Vaeloran's library and study (the party has been here).
Lower levels: Closed to visitors.
Known Layout
Ground floor: Reception, waiting area, Taren Myles's workspace
Upper floors: Vaeloran's library and study (party has been here)
Lower levels: Unknown to the party — heavily warded
Deepest chamber: The ritual room — Stage 3 shard attunement in progress
Notable Residents
Vaeloran lives and works here. His apprentice Lyssandra has returned to him after the meteor incident. His researcher Taren Myles can usually be found in the library.
DM — What's Really Here
Stage 3 ritual is active. The meteor shard sits in a specially prepared chamber surrounded by arcane anchors. Lyssandra assists as witness — she cannot enter this room of her own will but believes she chose to help. Taren Myles is increasingly uncomfortable but has not yet acted on it. The tower is warded with: Alarm (all entry points), Arcane Lock (lower levels), two Glyphs of Warding (dispelling and force), and Vaeloran's own Foresight if he is present.
Breaking In
Arcana DC 18: detect ritual energy from street level. Stealth DC 20: avoid Alarm wards on entry. Lower levels: Arcane Lock DC 25 (Dispel Magic DC 18 to bypass). The ritual room: additional ward triggers if shard is touched without Vaeloran's amulet.
What Can Be Found
Taren Myles's research notes — fragments of Vaeloran's plan in coded form (Investigation DC 16 to understand). The Aurek correspondence — letters signed with the alias. The shard (Stage 3 phylactery component). Lyssandra — who does not know she needs saving.
