Combat Behaviour & Tactics
Vaeloran does not want to fight the party. He wants to redirect them. Combat means something went wrong in his planning.

Round 1: Foresight is already active (cast this morning). Mind Blank is active. He has two Portent dice ready. He opens with Dominate Person on the most physically dangerous party member — if that's Torren, Torren is now his weapon.

Round 2: If Counterspell was needed, he uses Spell Mastery to recover it for free. Suggestion on Orin: "You know I'm trying to help this city. Stand down and let me explain." WIS DC 21.

Round 3+: He starts talking while the dominated party member fights their friends. "I didn't want this. You forced this." He is genuinely disappointed, not angry.

Escape condition: Below 60HP he casts Misty Step (free, Spell Mastery) and teleports to the Ethereal Plane via Third Eye. He does not die in this tower. He has contingencies for everything.

1st Age — Creation and Harmony (≈1,000 years)

Luminos and Veridania collaborated to shape Eldoria. Luminos contributed celestial energies — light, order, structure, the sun, stars, celestial realms. Veridania infused the world with nature, life, balance, plants, animals, natural ecosystems. The Pantheon of deities coexisted peacefully; each contributed to balance and prosperity.

Seeds of Ambition. As time passed, Astronar and Naturus grew discontented. Astronar coveted the power darkness and secrets could offer. Naturus sought to unleash chaos and decay to assert dominance.

Astronar's Manipulation. Astronar manipulated the emotions and desires of certain deities. He fostered mistrust, encouraged ambition, whispered lies about Luminos and Veridania's intentions. He planted seeds of doubt across the Pantheon — slowly, patiently, never showing his hand.

Naturus's Corruption. Naturus spread decay and chaos across Eldoria. He tainted ecosystems, turned bountiful lands into wastelands. His followers revelled in destruction.

The Usurpation — End of the 1st Age

At the end of the First Age, Astronar and Naturus openly challenged Luminos and Veridania. In a cataclysmic battle, the usurpers weakened the prime deities through deception, manipulation, and brute force. They drove Luminos and Veridania into hiding. Their power was significantly diminished.

The Downfall of the Dwarves of the Underpeak. During the Usurpation, the Aurum Dominion covertly infiltrated the Dwarven clans of the Underpeak — exploiting internal divisions and economic pressures over years:

  • Discovered rare enchanted crystals deep within the Underpeak.
  • Agents infiltrated communities posing as merchants and scholars.
  • Spread rumours, sowed discord between clans.
  • Economically isolated the clans by offering better deals to rivals.
  • Pressured neighbouring regions to withhold support.
  • When diplomacy failed, used military force and crystal magic superiority.
  • Some clans submitted; others who refused faced annihilation.

The result: Dwarves who refused submission began a great exodus from their ancestral homeland approximately two thousand years ago.

The Rise of the Celestium Clans. The exiled Dwarves settled beneath the Celestial Plateau. They carved intricate tunnels and halls into the rock. The proximity to celestial energies infused the environment with unique magic. They renamed themselves the Celestium Clans in recognition of their new home. They became skilled artificers, the plateau's protectors and guardians of celestial balance.

2nd Age — Dark Reign (≈750 years)

Astronar's Darkness. The sun's radiance was diminished. Perpetual twilight settled over Eldoria. Darkness extended into the hearts of mortals as fear and despair spread.

Naturus's Corruption. Natural ecosystems twisted into grotesque forms. Once-lush forests became mazes of thorns. Rivers polluted with foul substances. Predator-prey balance disrupted.

Tyranny of the Usurpers. Astronar and Naturus ruled with an iron fist. Absolute loyalty was demanded. Resisters were punished. Cults of darkness flourished with forbidden rituals.

The Lethraliri Civilisation. While the rest of Eldoria suffered, the Lethraliri civilisation thrived in what would become the Frostwood Marsh. Skilled in ice and water magic. They built stilt settlements above the water with interconnected platforms and pathways. Their cities were known for translucent ice sculptures and bioluminescent flora. They communicated with the marsh's elemental spirits — and they would prove instrumental at the end of the Age.

Whispered Resistance. Hidden followers of Luminos and Veridania whispered guidance through dreams. Secret networks formed. Heroes emerged under the banner of light.

The Order of the Silver Crescent. Founded during this period of celestial upheaval. Their teachings came from Lunara's visions, emphasising the importance of maintaining balance between light and darkness.

Heroes Rise. Mizura's followers unravelled the lies of the usurpers. Sylvana guided nomads and adventurers to organise resistance. Aetherius's prophecies inspired hope. Solara's light rekindled hope. Lunara's revelations exposed truths behind the reign.

The Great Reclamation — End of the 2nd Age

The united faithful embarked on a grand quest:

  • Yggdris healed the land.
  • Korvain crafted artifacts of light.
  • Sirenna's followers disrupted the usurpers' dominion.
  • Thalasia's rituals counteracted dark magic.
  • Mor'nyx guided the spirits of fallen heroes.

In a climactic battle, the combined might of deities and mortals proved overwhelming. Astronar and Naturus were cast down from their stolen thrones. Their followers turned against them.

The Dawnoak. Luminos and Veridania, exhausted but resolved, grew the Dawnoak — a divine tree of pure light (Luminos's domain) and pure root (Veridania's domain), rooted in places mortals cannot reach — and bound the Betrayer Gods within it. The Lethraliri were summoned to assist: they wrote the rituals, inscribed the binding's true name in their own tongue, and held the structure in place. As Astronar was drawn in, he went silent and submitted, plotting a longer game. As Naturus was drawn in, he resisted — and with his final reach hurled one of his unfinished children, Vroth-Khorn, through a tear in the planes into the material world.

The Lethraliri Sacrifice. Vroth-Khorn struck the land where the Lethraliri lived, gouging a wound that became the Frostwood Marsh. The Lethraliri could not destroy it — even a half-formed child of a god was too close to divine. So they sealed Vroth-Khorn beneath the marsh in unbreaking ice and froze themselves in place as living wardens. The Frozen Spire is the keystone. Their entire civilisation ended in that act.

Valdris the Pale Archivist. Not all First-Age threats were resolved cleanly. Valdris — a powerful First-Age lich — was not destroyed. The Aurum Dominion made a deal: they would protect the location of his phylactery and leave him undisturbed in his Aurora Peaks sanctum, in exchange for occasional arcane consultation. This arrangement has lasted three hundred years.

3rd Age — Restoration and Renewal (300 years · current era)

Restoration. With the defeat of Astronar and Naturus, balance gradually restored. Luminos and Veridania emerged from hiding. All deities joined forces to mend the damage. Eldoria began to heal.

The Divine Gate. All deities made a collective decision: to remove their physical forms from the material plane entirely, to prevent another usurpation. They sealed themselves behind the Divine Gate. This was a voluntary, collective sacrifice — not an imprisonment. The Dawnoak's binding holds; its true name was deliberately forgotten by every scholar who knew it, so that no being might ever speak it.

The Current Era. Three hundred years of relative peace and rebuilding. Independent city-states, merchant republics, competing empires. The Aurum Dominion remains expansionist. Aeloria Crossroads emerged as the neutral hub of the continent. Magic is common but never overwhelming — without divine guidance, mortals have shaped their own paths.

The Meteor Shards. Recently, a meteor struck north of Aeloria — in the wooded Aelorian Territories, about a day's travel from the city. The shards carry unusual arcane energy. Vaeloran sent his apprentice Lyssandra to retrieve one. The party found her there. The shards amplify magic significantly and are key components of Vaeloran's lich ritual. (DM-only: they are also fragments of Astronar's prison — each one shed through the Dawnoak's binding.)

DM NOTE — the campaign endgame. Vaeloran is collecting meteor shards to fuel his lichdom; he does not know they are fragments of Astronar's prison. Kaelith spreads Naturus's rot believing she's serving her god; she does not know her work weakens the Frostwood Marsh seal on Vroth-Khorn. The true campaign-ending threat is anyone who discovers the Dawnoak's true name — or who frees Vroth-Khorn, which would give Naturus a foothold in the material plane he has not had in 1,050 years.

Combat Strategy
How to Run Kaelith at the Table
Round 1: Cast Silence (120ft range) centred on Orin — removes his spellcasting entirely. Use Channel Divinity: Plague Blessing, choosing herself or a minion as the vector.

Round 2: Spirit Guardians on herself (WIS 17 save, 3d8 radiant/necrotic per turn to nearby enemies). Move into melee range — Cloak of Displacement gives attackers disadvantage until she takes damage.

Round 3+: Mace +3 attacks. Let Plague Blessing (exhaustion) and Spirit Guardians do the work. Use Virulent Burst as a Reaction whenever an enemy drops to 0HP — chains the damage.

Escape condition: Below 40HP she casts Misty Step / Gaseous Form and withdraws, leaving minions behind. She does not die unnecessarily.

Weakness: CHA -1, INT +1. Low social skills. If players can get Dispel Magic on Spirit Guardians and break Silence, she becomes much more manageable. Concentration on Spirit Guardians is her most important resource to protect.
