The Gnoll Den and What Lay Beneath
Hired by Oskar, the Goliath Townmaster of Aeloria, you were sent to deal with gnolls threatening the southeastern farmlands. You tracked them to their den and put an end to the pack.

But what you found inside changed everything.

A shrine — not to a gnoll deity, but to Naturus, the Betrayer God of rot and decay. The pack leader had been charmed and marked by an outside hand. And writings on the walls spoke of a larger plan: rot spreading through the Aelorian territories, guided by unseen hands. One note pointed to a location you already knew well: The Pig's Head Inn.

Back in Aeloria, the party split. Torren returned to O'Leary's Stables to check on Matilda — only to find her gone, spooked and escaped. Only tracks remained, heading north.

At the Pig's Head, Orin pressed patrons for information about a name: Merric. A missing friend. A past not yet buried. Through connections to a figure called Rulden, Torren gained access to a private meeting — and the truth turned out to be deeply personal. Rulden is Merric.

The next morning, a message arrived for Orin. Cold. Careful. Signed only "R." It ended:

"Keep to the light, Orin. It suits you better."

Orin sought guidance at the Temple of Luminos. Dawnwarden Seraphine listened — and gave her counsel: if Merric once walked in the light, and if you feel called to him still, then you should not stop.

The party now stands at Kolt's Depot. A caravan to Ironhold awaits. But behind you, threads remain unresolved — and a hidden network continues to spread rot through the land.

The Shard and the Letters
You were brought together by Vaeloran Duskwhisper, the archmage of Aeloria — a respected scholar-mage known for his charitable works and civic dedication. He set you two tasks: recover his missing apprentice, Lyssandra Vale, and deliver a set of sealed letters to members of Aeloria's ruling council on his behalf.

You rode north out of Aeloria on the trail of the meteor — about a day's travel into the wooded territories where the rock had struck. The clearing felt wrong: strange energies in the air, wildlife behaving oddly. You fought through corruption-warped spiders to reach a lonely barn nearby, where Lyssandra had been hiding.

She was afraid. She tried to burn the barn and flee rather than be brought back. Before disappearing into the night, she warned you:

"He'll twist your good intentions… like he did mine."
"That shard… it's not just a rock. He's been studying fragments like it for years."

You tracked her north through the wilds for five days, catching her on the road before she could reach Stonegate. She was brought back to Vaeloran, who thanked you warmly and said the shard had corrupted her mind — and that he would see to her recovery. The letters were delivered separately in the chaos.

Key Locations in Aeloria
Spire of the Silver Eclipse
Vaeloran's tower · High Spires District
An iconic tower in the heart of Aeloria. Its interior feels larger than it looks from outside. Swirling orbs of light, mechanical quills that write on their own, shelves that slide to make way as you pass. A faint scent of old paper and candlewax.
The Wayfarer's Rest
Inner trade ward · Respectable inn
Polished dark wood, soft lighting, private booths. The kind of place where merchants close deals and scholars draft letters. Sarah runs it with firm, quiet efficiency. A safe place to think.
The Pig's Head Inn
Outer trade ring · Working-class
Loud, crowded, cheap ale, good gossip. The kind of place where caravan guards, off-duty watchmen, and people with questions no one else will answer tend to end up. Gerald knows everyone and everything — at a price.
Temple of Luminos
White stone · Copper Light Ward
White stone with veins of faint gold. Tall windows catch the light. The air smells of incense and clean linen. Peaceful, orderly, and quietly watchful. Seraphine and the Lightbearer serve here.
Grand Bazaar
Central district · Market
Canvas awnings, shouting merchants, the smell of spice, metal, and baked bread. If it exists in Eldoria, someone here is selling it. Twelve established shops and stalls spread across the central district.
